Case 03

WorkRight Navigator

in collaboration with leading UK employment-rights advocates

The Problem

Every year, thousands of people are fired and suspect discrimination — because of disability, race, gender, age, faith, or other protected characteristics. Most have no lawyer, no understanding of the process, and in many cases limited literacy or cognitive capacity.

The UK system is complex: you can't go straight to court; you must go through ACAS Early Conciliation, watch strict time limits, and prepare structured information.

For someone already overwhelmed — shocked by dismissal, living with a disability, or struggling with forms — the system becomes inaccessible by design.

As a result, countless valid claims are never made, and discrimination goes unchallenged.

The Challenge

Build an AI-powered tool that helps vulnerable individuals understand and navigate their rights after being fired.

01 Clarity — give users a simple overview of the journey: what steps exist, what each step means, and what happens next
02 Accessibility — make the experience understandable for users with cognitive disabilities, low literacy, or high stress. Plain language, visual pathways, voice options, and adaptive support modes
03 Preparation Support — help users organise the basics: timeline, key events, what to tell ACAS, and what documents matter — so they can approach advisors better prepared

Constraints

Human-in-the-loop is mandatory. This tool must support — not replace — legal professionals, ACAS staff, or advice services.
Avoid legal advice. The system guides understanding and preparation, not courtroom strategy.
Design for vulnerability. Assume the user is overwhelmed, distrustful of institutions, and may struggle with memory, reading, or decision-making.
Scope realistically. A simple prototype that clearly explains "What happens after you're fired?" is more valuable than a full claim automation system.

Potential Use Cases

Rights Explorer
A guided walkthrough that helps users understand if what happened to them might be discrimination, using plain language and real examples
Timeline Builder
Help users reconstruct and document what happened, when, and who was involved — in a format advisors can use
ACAS Prep Assistant
Explain what Early Conciliation is, what to expect, and help users prepare what they want to say
Deadline Tracker
Show the critical time limits (usually 3 months minus 1 day) and send reminders before they expire
