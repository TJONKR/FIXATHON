Case 03

WorkRight Navigator

employment rights for vulnerable workers

The Problem

Every year, thousands of people are fired and suspect discrimination — because of disability, race, gender, age, faith, or other protected characteristics. Most have no lawyer, no understanding of the process, and in many cases limited literacy or cognitive capacity.

Every country has employment protection laws. And in every country, the system is complex: mandatory conciliation steps, strict time limits, structured documentation requirements, and bureaucratic processes that assume you already understand them.

For someone already overwhelmed — shocked by dismissal, living with a disability, or struggling with forms — the system becomes inaccessible by design.

As a result, countless valid claims are never made, and discrimination goes unchallenged.

The Challenge

Build an AI-powered tool that helps vulnerable individuals understand and navigate their rights after being fired. Pick a jurisdiction you know (UK, Netherlands, Germany, etc.) and make it work for that system.

01 Clarity — give users a simple overview of the journey: what steps exist, what each step means, and what happens next
02 Accessibility — make the experience understandable for users with cognitive disabilities, low literacy, or high stress. Plain language, visual pathways, voice options, and adaptive support modes
03 Preparation Support — help users organise the basics: timeline, key events, what to tell the conciliation body, and what documents matter — so they can approach advisors better prepared

Constraints

Human-in-the-loop is mandatory. This tool must support — not replace — legal professionals, conciliation staff, or advice services.
Avoid legal advice. The system guides understanding and preparation, not courtroom strategy.
Design for vulnerability. Assume the user is overwhelmed, distrustful of institutions, and may struggle with memory, reading, or decision-making.
Scope realistically. A simple prototype that clearly explains "What happens after you're fired?" for one country is more valuable than a multi-jurisdiction system that doesn't work.

Example: UK System

To give you a concrete reference, here's how the UK system works:

ACAS Early Conciliation — before going to tribunal, you must contact ACAS (Advisory, Conciliation and Arbitration Service) to attempt resolution
Strict time limits — typically 3 months minus 1 day from the discriminatory act to file
Protected characteristics — disability, race, gender, age, religion, sexual orientation, pregnancy, marriage status
Employment Tribunal — if conciliation fails, cases go to tribunal where you present evidence

Similar bodies exist elsewhere: College voor de Rechten van de Mens (NL), Antidiskriminierungsstelle (DE), Défenseur des droits (FR), etc.

Potential Use Cases

Rights Explorer
A guided walkthrough that helps users understand if what happened to them might be discrimination, using plain language and real examples
Timeline Builder
Help users reconstruct and document what happened, when, and who was involved — in a format advisors can use
Process Guide
Explain what conciliation means in your jurisdiction, what to expect, and help users prepare what they want to say
Deadline Tracker
Show the critical time limits for your jurisdiction and send reminders before they expire
