Case 02

LUMC

Department of Clinical Epidemiology

The Problem

Researchers are buried in admin. The actual science gets squeezed into whatever time is left.

The Department of Clinical Epidemiology at Leiden University Medical Center exists to answer clinically relevant questions. 75 people — professors, post-docs, PhD candidates — working to improve healthcare through research.

But research has a bureaucracy problem. Before you can study anything, you're cleaning data, reviewing literature, writing protocols, coordinating teams, hunting for that presentation someone gave two years ago. Poor data quality compromises entire studies. Systematic reviews take months. Knowledge gets lost in messy folders.

This isn't unique to LUMC. Every research institution faces it. The ones who solve it will move faster, publish more, and ultimately save more lives.

The Challenge

Free researchers from administrative gravity. Build AI-powered tools that give them their time back — so they can focus on the work that matters.

01 Data management — automate cleaning, validation, and quality checks that currently eat weeks
02 Literature synthesis — accelerate systematic reviews and evidence gathering
03 Knowledge retrieval — make years of institutional knowledge findable and usable
04 Workflow automation — reduce coordination overhead so teams can focus on research

Constraints

No patient data. Privacy first, always. Work with synthetic or anonymized data only.
Local deployment. Solutions must run on-premise. No cloud dependencies for sensitive workflows.
Python preferred. The department works in Python. Integration matters.

Potential Use Cases

The Knowledge Center
Turn years of buried presentations and research into a searchable, structured archive with AI-powered summaries
Meetings → Actions
Real-time transcription that extracts action items, generates minutes, and sends tasks automatically
The Protocol Assistant
AI co-pilot that turns spoken research discussions into structured protocol documents in real-time
The Meeting Orchestrator
Self-service scheduling with auto-reminders and gap-filling when speakers cancel
