Case 04

Food Waste

Norsken — Food Waste Redistribution

The Problem

Every day, tons of perfectly good food gets thrown away while people go hungry. Not because we lack food. Because we can't get it to where it's needed — fast enough.

Supermarkets pull products days before they expire. Restaurants dump what doesn't sell. Meanwhile, food banks scramble to fill gaps. The infrastructure exists — food banks, community fridges, apps like Too Good To Go. But the matching is chaos.

Timing is brutal. Logistics are fragmented. Trust is complicated. Data doesn't flow. We throw away what we could share, and spend money on both disposal AND hunger relief — separately.

The Challenge

Build a solution that gets surplus food to people who need it — before it becomes waste.

01 Real-time matching — connect surplus to demand as it happens, not hours later
02 Logistics coordination — solve the "who picks up, who delivers, who's available now" problem
03 Predictive surplus — help businesses see waste coming before it happens
04 Trust infrastructure — handle safety, liability, verification for donors and recipients

Constraints

Time kills. Food expires. Your solution has to work faster than decay.
Low-tech users. Bakery owners, shelter volunteers — not everyone has a smartphone in hand.
Trust is non-negotiable. If donors worry about liability or recipients about safety, they won't participate.

Potential Use Cases

Surplus Alert System
One-tap notification from restaurants to nearby food banks when surplus is available
Volunteer Dispatch
Match available volunteer drivers to pickup/delivery routes in real-time
Waste Predictor
AI analyzes sales patterns to forecast surplus before it happens
Food Bank Demand Board
Let shelters broadcast what they need so donors can match proactively
